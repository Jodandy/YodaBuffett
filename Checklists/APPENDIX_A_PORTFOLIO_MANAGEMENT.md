APPENDIX A: PORTFOLIO MANAGEMENT RULES
═══════════════════════════════════════════CASH MANAGEMENTPhilosophy:
"Cash is a position. Being patient is an action."Guidelines:Comfortable Cash Levels:

Normal market: 20-30% cash
Elevated valuations: 30-50% cash
Market uncertainty/volatility: 40-60% cash
Finding nothing attractive: 50%+ cash is fine
Never force investments:

Don't invest in "pretty good" opportunities just to be invested
Wait for genuine fat pitches (50-100%+ upside)
Missing opportunities is better than losing capital
Cash Management:

Keep in short-term vehicles earning 3-5%

Money market funds
Short-term Treasury bills
High-yield savings accounts


Maintain liquidity for quick deployment
No more than $250K per institution (FDIC limits)
POSITION MANAGEMENTPortfolio Size:
Total positions: 8-15 companiesWhy this range:

<8 positions: Too concentrated, single-company risk too high
8-12 positions: Sweet spot for active management
13-15 positions: Maximum before becoming index-like


15 positions: Too many to monitor properly


Quality over quantity:

Better to have 8 great companies than 15 good ones
Each position should be a genuine conviction
If you can't monitor it properly, don't own it
Position Size Guidelines:Maximum Limits:

Single position: 15% (including appreciation)
Sector: 25% across all positions
Geography: 40% in any single country
Emerging markets: 20% total
Turnarounds: 10% total
Why these limits:

Prevents catastrophic portfolio damage from single failure
Forces diversification across themes
Allows concentration in best ideas
Protects against sector-specific crashes
Position Maintenance Rules:Review positions <2% for exit:

Why? If position has declined to <2%, either:

Thesis is broken (should exit)
Or never should have bought it (exit)


Small positions don't move the needle
Take tax loss and redeploy elsewhere
Trim positions >15%:

When position appreciates beyond 15% due to gains:

Trim back to 12-13%
Take profits and rebalance
Exception: If conviction remains very high and fundamentals strengthening


Rebalance quarterly:

Review all positions
Check if sizes still match conviction
Trim winners, potentially add to laggards (if thesis intact)
Maintain target allocations
SCALING STRATEGYBuilding Positions:Initial Entry:

Start at lower end of calculated size range
Example: If sizing at 5-7%, start with 5%
Allows room to add if thesis strengthens
When to Add More:✅ Add on fundamental strength:

Business performing better than expected
New products/markets succeeding
Margins improving
Management executing well
Fundamentals strengthening
✅ Add on technical weakness:

Stock drops 15%+ on no fundamental news
Market overreacts to temporary issue
Better risk/reward at lower prices
Thesis remains intact
✅ Add on new catalysts:

New positive developments emerge
Unexpected opportunities appear
Competitive position strengthens
Regulatory wins
✅ Add on working capital improvement:

DSO declining
Cash flow conversion improving
Major payments received
✅ Add on culture improvement:

New management team
Glassdoor ratings improving
Key hires made
When NOT to Add:❌ Never add if:

Fundamental thesis weakening
DSO increasing quarter-over-quarter
Cash flow deteriorating
Management announces unexpected capital raise
Major customer issues emerge
Insider selling accelerates
Short seller report with credible concerns
Position already at maximum size
Better opportunities exist elsewhere
Cultural deterioration visible
The "Falling Knife Rule":If stock has fallen >50% from recent peak:
Wait 8-12 weeks for stabilization

Let the dust settle
Allow market to find a base
See if other buyers emerge



Wait for evidence of improvement

Not just management promises
Actual data showing progress
Sequential improvement in metrics



Wait for insider buying

Management buying at low prices
Significant amounts ($100K+)
Multiple insiders buying



Then consider small add (1-2%) if thesis intact
Do NOT catch falling knives hoping for bounce.AVERAGING DOWN RULESWhen Averaging Down Makes Sense:✅ Stock drops 15%+ without fundamental deterioration:

PORTFOLIO MONITORINGDaily (5 minutes):

 Check prices
 Scan news headlines
 Review alerts
Weekly (15 minutes):

 Review any material news
 Check for insider trading (Form 4s)
 Monitor position sizes
 Scan for better opportunities
Monthly (1 hour):

 Calculate DSO for positions with working capital risk
 Check Glassdoor ratings
 Review competitive news
 Update thesis if needed
 Rebalance if necessary
Quarterly (2-3 hours per position):

 Read 10-Q filing
 Listen to earnings call
 Review conference call transcript
 Update financial models
 Reassess conviction level
 Check if position size still appropriate
 Write quarterly review note
PORTFOLIO HEALTH CHECKSRed Flags (Review immediately):Position-specific:

Any position down >25% since purchase
Any position with DSO increasing >20 days
Any position with management changes
Any position with negative news
Portfolio-level:

Cash below 15% (getting too aggressive)
Single position >18% (too concentrated)
Sector >30% (too concentrated)
3+ positions underperforming simultaneously
Green Flags (Good signs):Position-specific:

Position up >50% and fundamentals improving
DSO declining, cash flow improving
Insider buying
Positive unexpected news
Portfolio-level:

Cash 20-40% (balanced)
All positions within size limits
Good sector diversification
Conviction level matches position sizes
