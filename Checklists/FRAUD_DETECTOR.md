## PATTERN RECOGNITION LIBRARY

### Red Flag Patterns to Avoid:

#### Pattern 1: "Vendor Financing Death Spiral"

**Characteristics:**
- Gross margins >60%, EBIT margins >40%
- Revenue growing >100% YoY
- Operating cash flow conversion <50%
- DSO >120 days and increasing
- Extended payment terms discussed as "strategic"
- Frequent capital raises despite "profitability"

**Why it fails:**
- Company is financing customer purchases
- Customers don't value product at stated price
- Eventually run out of capital to lend
- Receivables prove uncollectible
- Company collapses when music stops

**Historical examples:** 
- Intellego (-82%)
- Quindell (-99.7%)

**Action:** Never invest

---

#### Pattern 2: "China Revenue Mirage"

**Characteristics:**
- >30% revenue from unverifiable Chinese customers
- "Framework agreements" worth billions with Chinese entities
- Partnership with Chinese distributor you can't verify
- Non-Big-4 auditor or weak auditor presence
- Management defensive when asked about verification

**Why it fails:**
- Revenue may not exist at all
- Chinese "customers" may be shells
- Framework agreements never convert to real sales
- Auditor unable or unwilling to verify
- Eventually exposed by short sellers or regulators

**Historical examples:** 
- Luckin Coffee (-97%)
- Sino-Forest (-99%)
- Multiple Chinese reverse merger frauds

**Action:** Avoid entirely OR max 1-2% speculation only

---

#### Pattern 3: "Roll-Up Collapse"

**Characteristics:**
- 5+ acquisitions in 2 years
- Organic growth <10% while reported growth >50%
- Announcing new acquisitions during operational problems
- Rising debt/equity to fund M&A
- Integration problems visible
- Each acquisition "synergistic" but margins declining

**Why it fails:**
- Hiding organic decline with acquisition growth
- Overpaying for acquisitions (desperation)
- Unable to integrate acquisitions
- Accounting games across entities
- Eventually run out of acquisition targets or capital

**Historical examples:** 
- Valeant (-96%)
- Quindell (-99.7%)

**Action:** Avoid unless strong organic growth verified

---

#### Pattern 4: "Peak Capital Raise"

**Characteristics:**
- Stock at/near all-time highs
- Major capital raise (>20% dilution)
- Aggressive new revenue targets announced simultaneously
- Insider selling occurring around same time
- Working capital problems not disclosed

**Why it fails:**
- Raising capital because desperate, not opportunistic
- Aggressive targets to justify high valuation
- Insiders know problems coming
- Stock price drops after raise
- Dilution + declining business = double negative

**Historical examples:** 
- Multiple (pattern repeats frequently)

**Action:** Exit 50%+ if you own, never initiate new position

---

#### Pattern 5: "Management Credibility Collapse"

**Characteristics:**
- First: Admits "communication challenges"
- Then: CFO or auditor change
- Then: Guidance reduced or accounting questions
- Finally: Investigation, lawsuit, or fraud revealed

**Why it fails:**
- Credibility issues are iceberg (tip visible, mass hidden)
- One lie leads to uncovering more
- Auditor/CFO change signals deep problems
- Market loses trust completely

**Historical examples:** 
- Wirecard (-99%)
- Luckin Coffee (-97%)

**Action:** Exit immediately on first credibility warning

---

#### Pattern 6: "Framework Agreement Fantasy"

**Characteristics:**
- Massive "framework agreements" or "MOUs" announced
- Potential value 5-10x current annual revenue
- Dependent on regulatory approval or future conditions
- No binding purchase commitments
- Used to justify stock price or capital raise
- Previous framework agreements didn't materialize

**Why it fails:**
- Framework ≠ binding contract
- Used to pump stock price
- Rarely convert to actual revenue
- Meanwhile, actual business deteriorating

**Historical examples:**
- Multiple companies (common tactic)

**Action:** Ignore completely for valuation. Only value actual binding orders.

---

### Green Flag Pattern (What Good Companies Look Like):

#### "The Compounding Cash Machine"

**Characteristics:**
- Operating cash flow consistently >90% of net income
- DSO stable and <60 days
- Organic revenue growth 15-30% annually
- Margins stable or improving
- Minimal capital requirements for growth
- Management underpromises and overdelivers
- Consistent insider buying
- No capital raises needed
- Returns cash to shareholders (buybacks/dividends)

**Why it succeeds:**
- Real business with real customers
- Product valued at stated price
- Management conservative and credible
- Business model works
- Capital efficient growth
- Time compounds value

**Examples:** 
- Quality SaaS businesses
- Platform businesses with network effects
- Recurring revenue models
- Asset-light businesses

**Action:** These are the companies worth 10-15% positions
