## What They Do

4C Group is a Swedish defense technology company (founded 2000, IPO'd 2022 at SEK 35) that develops **Exonaut**, a software platform for military training and exercise management. The platform covers planning, execution, evaluation, wargaming, and readiness reporting. They also have a smaller resilience/BCM offering for corporate clients, but are actively pivoting away from that toward pure defense focus.

## Why It's Interesting

- **Niche dominance:** Exonaut is described as the "de facto standard" for military exercise management software, with NATO accreditation at Secret and Unclassified levels. Janes (defense intelligence publication) called it potentially "the most significant piece of software that no one has heard of."
- **Customer list:** NATO, British Army, US Army, US Marine Corps, Canadian Army, Swedish, Norwegian, Australian, Swiss, and Finnish armed forces.
- **No direct competitor:** Alternative lists show only generic GRC tools — no dedicated military exercise management rival at scale.
- **20+ years of user-driven development** creates a meaningful product moat.
- **Defense tailwind:** European rearmament wave and US DoD modernization create strong macro backdrop. Defense now 77% of revenue with 70% of ARR growth from this segment.
- **Valuation:** P/S below 1, market cap ~SEK 400M ($39M USD).

## The Bull Case

ARR of SEK 250M growing steadily, order book up 50%, software mix improving. If revenue reaches SEK 400-450M at 75%+ software mix and they hit 20% EBIT margins, that's SEK 80-90M operating profit on a SEK 400M market cap — implying a 4-5x P/E for a monopoly-position defense SaaS company. The operating leverage clearly exists (Q4 margins of 21-23% prove it).

## The Bear Case

- IPO'd at SEK 35, now trading at SEK 9-12 — down ~70%.
- Full-year operating cash flow is **negative and worsening**.
- 20% organic growth target vs. 7% actual — management under-delivering.
- Dilutive share issue (SEK 55M raised in 2025).
- Increasing net debt funded via overdraft facility.
- TAM may be limited — finite number of allied armed forces, lumpy multi-year contracts.
- Expert services drag (26% of revenue at lower margins) may be structural.
- 25 years old and still not cash-flow positive on a full-year basis.

## Verdict

The product is genuinely strong and the niche position is real, but the financials don't support a fat pitch today. The company is a *potential* cash cow that hasn't yet demonstrated it can actually get there. Too much "trust management" in the story when management has consistently under-delivered.

**Trigger to revisit:** One or two quarters where full-year operating cash flow turns positive while ARR keeps compounding. That would confirm the operating leverage thesis and could make this actionable.
