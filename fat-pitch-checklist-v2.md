Fat Pitch Investment Checklist (Updated with Culture Assessment)
Core Philosophy Reminder
"If the risk/reward isn't compelling enough to justify meaningful capital allocation, it's not worth doing."

Goal: Concentrated positions in exceptional opportunities with 50-100%+ upside potential over 1-3 years through fundamental analysis enhanced by market timing awareness and operational excellence assessment.

Pre-Investment Checklist
1. Opportunity Assessment
Upside Potential:

 Can I realistically see 50-100%+ upside over 1-3 years from current price?
 Is the addressable market large enough to support this growth?
 Are there clear catalysts that could drive this performance?
 Is this a secular trend or cyclical opportunity?
 Does the current valuation still offer attractive risk/reward despite any recent moves?
Risk/Reward Ratio:

 Is the downside limited relative to upside potential?
 What's the worst-case scenario and can I live with it?
 Am I being compensated adequately for the risks taken?
 Have I considered both fundamental and technical risks?
2. Business Quality Assessment
Competitive Position:

 Does the company have a sustainable competitive advantage (moat)?
 Is management competent and aligned with shareholders?
 Are the financials healthy (or improving if turnaround story)?
 Is the business model scalable and capital-efficient?
Market Position:

 Is this a leader in their niche or have potential to become one?
 Are they gaining or losing market share?
 Do they have pricing power?
3. Culture & Management Assessment
Employee Sentiment Analysis:

 Glassdoor Rating: >3.5/5 preferred, <3.0/5 is concerning
 Recommendation Rate: >60% would recommend to friend
 Leadership Rating: CEO/Senior Management >3.5/5
 Trend Direction: Ratings improving or stable over 12+ months
 Review Velocity: Steady stream of reviews (not just disgruntled departures)
Management Quality Indicators:

 Insider Alignment: Management buying stock, not just selling
 Track Record: Consistent guidance achievement over 2+ years
 Communication Style: Professional, transparent investor relations
 Operational Excellence: No recent FDA warnings, quality issues
 Retention: Low executive turnover in key operational roles
Cultural Red Flags (Avoid if multiple present):

 Toxic Leadership: Multiple reviews citing CEO/management behavior issues
 High Turnover: Glassdoor mentions of frequent firings, job insecurity
 Compensation Issues: Below-market pay, no raises during profitable periods
 Regulatory Risk: Manufacturing quality issues + cultural problems
 Execution Risk: Toxic culture + aggressive growth targets
Position Sizing Adjustments for Culture Risk:

Green Culture (>3.5 Glassdoor, stable leadership): No adjustment
Yellow Culture (2.5-3.5 rating, some concerns): Reduce position size by 1-2%
Red Culture (<2.5 rating, multiple red flags): Reduce by 3-4% or avoid
4. Timing and Technical Considerations
Market Context:

 Is the sector/market in favor or fighting major headwinds?
 Is this an appropriate time to enter, or should I wait for better setup?
 Are broader market conditions supportive of this type of investment?
 Is the stock in an uptrend, downtrend, or consolidation?
Entry Strategy:

 Should I enter full position or scale in gradually?
 Is there a catalyst timeline that affects my entry decision?
 Am I buying because of FOMO or genuine opportunity?
5. Conviction Test
Knowledge & Research:

 Have I done sufficient research to understand the business?
 Do I understand the key risks and how to monitor them?
 Can I explain the investment thesis in 2-3 sentences?
 Have I researched the competition and industry dynamics?
Personal Conviction:

 Am I willing to hold this through 25-30% volatility?
 Would I buy more if it dropped 20% on no fundamental news?
 Can I hold this for 1-3 years if thesis remains intact?
 Would I still buy this if I discovered it today at current price?
Position Sizing Framework
6. Dynamic Position Sizing
Starting Point - Conviction Level:

 High conviction (strong thesis, low uncertainty): Start at 8-12%
 Medium conviction (good opportunity, some uncertainty): Start at 5-7%
 Lower conviction (asymmetric bet, high uncertainty): Start at 2-4%
Volatility Adjustments:

 Low volatility (large stable companies): No adjustment
 Medium volatility (most growth stocks): Reduce by 1-2%
 High volatility (small growth, biotech, turnarounds): Reduce by 3-4%
Liquidity/Size Adjustments:

 Large cap (>$10B): No adjustment
 Mid cap ($1-10B): Reduce by 1%
 Small cap (<$1B): Reduce by 2-3%
Portfolio Context Adjustments:

 Current cash position: More cash = can size larger
 Sector concentration: Reduce if similar positions exist
 Market conditions: Reduce in volatile/uncertain periods
Culture Risk Adjustments (Apply after other adjustments):

 Green Culture (>3.5 Glassdoor): No additional adjustment
 Yellow Culture (2.5-3.5): Additional 1-2% reduction
 Red Culture (<2.5): Additional 3-4% reduction or avoid entirely
7. Scaling Strategy
Building Positions:

 Initial entry: Start at lower end of size range
 Add on fundamental strength: Business performing better than expected
 Add on technical weakness: Stock drops without fundamental deterioration
 Add on new catalysts: Additional positive developments
 Add on culture improvement: Management changes, Glassdoor ratings improving
Maximum Position Guidelines:

 Single position maximum: 15% (including appreciation)
 Sector maximum: 25% across all positions
 Geographic maximum: 40% in any single country/region
Risk Management Framework
8. Stop Loss Strategy (Choose Appropriate Method)
Thesis-Based Stops (Preferred for fundamental plays):

 Exit when original investment thesis is invalidated
 Monitor key business metrics and competitive position changes
 Best for: High conviction, quality businesses
Technical Stops (For market timing awareness):

 Large caps: 20-25% stops
 Small/volatile stocks: 30-40% stops
 Trend-following: Exit on major support breaks
Culture-Based Stops (New):

 Exit 25-50% if key executives depart unexpectedly
 Exit 50%+ if regulatory warnings + cultural deterioration
 Reduce position if Glassdoor rating drops >0.5 points
Hybrid Approach:

 Use wider technical stops (25-35%) combined with thesis monitoring
 Allow for normal volatility while protecting against major trend changes
9. Averaging Down Rules
When to Add More:

 Stock drops 15%+ without fundamental deterioration
 New positive developments strengthen the thesis
 Improved risk/reward at lower prices
 Have available cash and position isn't oversized
 Culture metrics stable or improving during weakness
When NOT to Average Down:

 Fundamental thesis has weakened
 Management issues or competitive threats emerge
 Already at maximum position size
 Better opportunities exist elsewhere
 Cultural deterioration occurring
Portfolio Management Rules
10. Portfolio Discipline
Cash Management:

 Comfortable holding 20-50% cash while waiting for opportunities
 Not forcing investments into "pretty good" opportunities
 Cash in appropriate short-term vehicles earning 3-4%
Position Management:

 Total positions: 8-15 (focus on quality over quantity)
 Review positions below 2% for potential exit
 Trim positions above 15% due to appreciation
 Rebalance based on changing conviction levels
11. Ongoing Monitoring Framework
Monthly Monitoring:

 Track key business metrics monthly/quarterly
 Monitor competitive position and industry trends
 Watch for better opportunities (opportunity cost)
 Culture Check: Glassdoor ratings, executive changes, regulatory updates
 Insider Activity: Track management buying/selling patterns
Quarterly Deep Dives:

 Conference call tone and management confidence
 Employee sentiment trend analysis
 Competitive intelligence and market share data
 Assess technical picture periodically
Exit Triggers:

 Thesis invalidation (fundamental deterioration)
 Better opportunity requiring capital
 Target price reached with limited further upside
 Major trend breakdown (technical exit)
 Position becomes oversized (>15%)
 Culture Collapse: Key talent exodus, regulatory issues, management misconduct
Final Decision Framework
The Ultimate Questions:
Is the risk/reward compelling enough to justify meaningful capital allocation?
Do I have genuine conviction in this opportunity based on thorough research?
Am I buying at an appropriate time given both fundamental and technical factors?
Can I size this appropriately given volatility, liquidity, and my current portfolio?
Would I buy this if I discovered it today for the first time?
Do I trust management to execute, and is the culture conducive to operational excellence? (NEW)
If all answers are "YES" → Proceed with appropriate position sizing If any answer is "NO" → Pass or wait for better conditions

Key Principles to Remember:
Quality over quantity - Better to own fewer exceptional businesses with strong cultures
Patience pays - Wait for genuine fat pitches, don't force investments
Size appropriately - Wins should move the needle, losses shouldn't devastate
Stay flexible - Adjust approach based on market conditions and opportunities
Think independently - Don't avoid stocks just because they've already moved
Manage risk actively - Use appropriate combination of sizing, stops, and monitoring
Culture matters - Toxic management can destroy great businesses faster than competition
People drive execution - The best strategies fail with the wrong people
